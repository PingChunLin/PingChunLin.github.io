Ping-Chun Lin

Research Scientist working on GeoAI and Data science at the University of Washington. Interested in Machine Learning, Remote Sensing, Computer Vision, Geospatial Artificial Intelligence.

Projects I worked on:
  • Lin et al., (2025) is a physics-informed diffusion-based SAR despeckling method that improves object detection.
  • Farmponds is a pipeline that uses pretrained computer vision models that help identify farm ponds to analyze climate-change research.
  • REE-pH is a application that estimates ocean pH in the past based on rare earth concentrations in seawater (see also Lin and Catling, 2024).
  • microSWIFT(checkout) is an application that helps researchers in the UW Applied Physics Lab set up bottle sized wave buoys.
  • Dry Valley Hillslope Flow Accumulation is a hydrologic analysis for the Dry Valleys in Antarctica (see also Toner et al., 2022).
